--- WHAT TO JUDGE ON EACH SCREEN ---

A) REAL-WORLD PRESENTATION (most important)
- Is this the CORRECT way to show this content to a real recruiter/client?
- Does it show the RIGHT things? Anything missing that hiring managers expect
  (tech stack, impact/metrics, role, outcomes, proof, CV/resume, contact)?
- Anything shown that shouldn't be, or that weakens credibility?
- Is the ORDER and EMPHASIS right — most important things first, scannable in seconds?
- Positioning: does it clearly say "senior .NET full-stack" and back it with proof?
- Trust & credibility: would a real hiring manager believe and be impressed?
- Does each project/work item make its value obvious (problem → what he did → result)?
- Too much prose, or too little substance? Right level of detail for a busy recruiter?

B) DESIGN
- First impression: premium and credible, or cheap/unfinished?
- Layout & alignment, spacing, consistent grid; nothing crooked or cramped.
- Typography: hierarchy, readability, sizes, weight, line length.
- Colour & contrast: brand consistency, enough contrast.
- Consistency across screens: do all pages feel like one designed product?
- Visual polish: borders, shadows, icons, buttons, hover states, animation.
- Check BOTH light and dark themes (toggle in the nav).
- Check desktop AND mobile (~390px).
